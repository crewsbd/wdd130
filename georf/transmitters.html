Transmitters

While a GMRS/FRS radio is almost always mobile, some radio transmissions and frequencies are associated with transmitters at specific locations. In amateur radio, there are many dedicated frequencies for devices called repeaters. These are used by licensed operators and allows them to extend their reach. Similarly, various government agencies have elaborate radio networks to enable them to efficiently communicate. When a police officer calls for backup, it likely will go to a nearby transceiver, get routed through a wired network, and get re-transmitted at a much more distant location. In an urban location, there could be hundreds of transmitters at work.

On GeoRF, you can search for and bookmark interesting stations on the map.
When you search for a frequency on GeoRF, it might also show you approximately where it is located. This can be handy in helping you know if you can reach it with whatever radio equipment you might have. Many radio enthusiasts use small USB dongles called SDRs(Software Defined Radio) to view and listen to a wide range or transmissions. Others use radio scanners to listen to municipal communications from police, medical and fire services.
